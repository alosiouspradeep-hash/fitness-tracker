Important Notes

Quad Focus Strategy: This program hits quads every day with varying intensity. Day 3 is your heavy quad day - go all out. On upper body days (1, 2, 4), quad work is lighter/moderate volume for recovery. Day 5 combines quads with posterior chain.

Recovery Tips: With daily quad training, prioritize sleep, protein intake (130g+/day), and foam rolling/stretching quads between sessions. If quads feel overtrained, reduce sets on upper body days. Listen to your body and adjust intensity as needed.

Protein Sources (No Beef)

Chicken, Turkey, Fish (Salmon, Tuna, Tilapia), Eggs, Greek Yogurt, Cottage Cheese, Whey Protein, Tofu, Lentils

Adjust Calories

Add 100-200 cal on heavy quad days (Day 3 & 5). Reduce carbs slightly on rest days. Keep protein constant.

Meal Timing

Eat protein within 2hrs post-workout. Space meals 3-4 hours apart. 30-40g protein per meal is optimal.

Hydration

Drink 500ml water with each meal. Total 3-4L daily. Add electrolytes if training hard.

🔥 Fat Loss Strategy (24% → Target 15%)

Caloric Deficit

Aim for 300-500 cal deficit. At 81kg, target ~2000-2200 kcal/day for steady fat loss while preserving muscle.

Protein Priority

Consume 160-180g protein daily (2-2.2g/kg) to maintain muscle during the cut. Spread across 4-5 meals.

Cardio Protocol

Add 2-3 sessions of 20-30min LISS (walking, cycling) on rest days. Avoid excessive cardio that impacts quad recovery.

Post-Workout

Consider 10-15 min incline treadmill walk after weights to boost calorie burn without affecting performance.

Important Notes

Quad Priority Strategy: This program is designed to bring up weak quads while maintaining your already strong upper body. Upper body volume is reduced to "maintenance" level (enough to keep gains, not over-train). Days 3 and 5 are dedicated heavy quad days.

Upper Body Note: Chest is on minimal volume (1 exercise/day) since it's already strong. Shoulders get more focus with 2-3 exercises per push day. Arms (biceps/triceps) get dedicated work on both push and pull days to maintain size while focusing recovery on quads.

Fat Loss + Muscle: At 24% body fat, you can effectively lose fat while building quad muscle (body recomposition). Keep protein high, maintain a moderate deficit, and prioritize sleep. Track progress with photos and measurements, not just scale weight.
